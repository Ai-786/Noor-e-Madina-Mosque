درود ابراہیم

Darood-e-Ibrahim

Source: Sahih al-Bukhari 3370 & Sahih Muslim 406 | Recited in every Salah

📖 Introduction

Darood Ibrahim is the most fundamental and universally recited form of Salawat (blessings) upon the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in the entire Islamic tradition. It is not merely a supplication but a pillar of worship — recited in the Qa'dah (sitting position) of every single Salah by hundreds of millions of Muslims worldwide, multiple times every day. Ka'b ibn Ujrah ؓ narrated that when the verse of Salawat (Al-Ahzab 33:56) was revealed, the Companions asked the Prophet ﷺ how they should send blessings upon him. The Prophet ﷺ himself taught them this very Darood, which has been transmitted through an unbroken chain of narration for over 1,400 years. Its spiritual power lies in comparing our invocation of blessings to those that Allah sent upon Ibrahim ﷺ — the Khalilullah (Friend of Allah) — reflecting the supreme station of our beloved Prophet ﷺ.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is Darood Ibrahim the same as Durood Ibrahim?
+
Yes — "Darood" (Urdu/Persian) and "Durood" are the same word, simply transliterated differently. "Durood Ibrahim" is the most widely used term in South Asian communities, while "Darood" is common in Pakistani and Indian usage. Both refer to the exact same supplication taught by the Prophet ﷺ himself.
Is it compulsory to recite Darood Ibrahim in every Salah?
+
According to the Hanafi school, Darood Ibrahim in the final Qa'dah (sitting) is Wajib (obligatory). In the Shafi'i, Maliki, and Hanbali schools, it is considered Fardh (a pillar) of the prayer. Neglecting it intentionally can invalidate the Salah according to several scholars.
What is the reward for reciting Darood Ibrahim once?
+
The Prophet ﷺ said: "Whoever sends blessings upon me once, Allah sends ten blessings upon him, erases ten sins, and raises him ten degrees." (Sahih Muslim 408). Each recitation earns at minimum 10-fold divine mercy.
What are the best times to recite Darood Ibrahim?
+
After every obligatory Salah, abundantly on Fridays (Jumu'ah), when entering and leaving the mosque, during Dua (at beginning and end), when the Prophet ﷺ is mentioned, and after hearing the Adhan. Friday is especially recommended — many scholars advise 200-1000 times on Jumu'ah.
Can I recite Darood Ibrahim outside of Salah?
+
Absolutely. The Prophet ﷺ encouraged abundant Salawat at all times. Many scholars recommend 100 times daily outside of Salah as a personal Wird (regular practice). There is no restriction on the number of times one may recite it.

Darood-e-Tanjeena (Tunjina)

Source: Vision of Shaykh Musa al-Darir | Recorded in Fajr al-Munir by Imam al-Fakahani (d.734 AH)

⛵ The Miraculous Story

Shaykh Musa al-Darir was on a ship caught in a deadly storm known as "al-iqlabiyya" (the Capsizer). In a blessed dream, the Prophet ﷺ himself appeared and taught him this Darood, saying: "Tell the passengers to recite it 1,000 times." By the time they reached 300 recitations, the storm had completely settled. This story is narrated by Shaykh Umar ibn Ali al-Fakahani in Fajr al-Munir and praised by Ibn Arabi and Imam al-Buni.

اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَىٰ سَيِّدِنَا مُحَمَّدٍ صَلَاةً تُنْجِينَا بِهَا مِنْ جَمِيعِ الْأَهْوَالِ وَالْآفَاتِ ❁ وَتَقْضِى لَنَا بِهَا جَمِيعَ الْحَاجَاتِ ❁ وَتُطَهِّرُنَا بِهَا مِنْ جَمِيعِ السَّيِّئَاتِ ❁ وَتَرْفَعُنَا بِهَا عِنْدَكَ أَعْلَى الدَّرَجَاتِ ❁ وَتُبَلِّغُنَا بِهَا أَقْصَى الْغَايَاتِ ❁ مِنْ جَمِيعِ الْخَيْرَاتِ ❁ فِي الْحَيَاةِ وَبَعْدَ الْمَمَاتِ

How many times should I recite Darood Tunjina for a specific need?
+
For pressing needs (Hajaat), scholars recommend 1,000 times in one sitting. For daily use: 11 times after each obligatory Salah. On Friday morning (between Sunnah and Fardh of Fajr): 1,000 times is especially powerful for urgent needs. The Prophet ﷺ said 1,000 recitations during adversity guarantees deliverance.
Is the story of the ship storm authentic?
+
Yes, the story is recorded by Imam Umar ibn Ali al-Fakahani (d. 734 AH) in his work "Fajr al-Munir." It is also referenced by Ibn Arabi, Imam al-Buni, and Shaykh Muhammad Haqqi al-Nazili. While some scholars note it comes through a dream (ru'ya), the majority of Sufi scholars consider it among the most reliable reports of its kind due to the consistent transmission and the tawatur of spiritual experience.

Family & Progeny

Darood-e-Lukhi is a highly beloved Salawat from the Qadiri tradition, widely transmitted through the scholars of the Indian subcontinent. It is known for its conciseness combined with immense spiritual depth, making it ideal for frequent recitation as a daily Wird (regular practice).

O Allah, send blessings and peace upon our master Muhammad, the unlettered Prophet, and upon his family and companions.
📿
Easy for Daily Wird
Short and powerful — ideal for 100x, 500x, or 1000x recitation as a daily spiritual practice.
🤲
Companions Included

⏰ Recommended: 100x daily as morning Wird. 1000x on Friday for immense spiritual reward.

🕌 About the Mosque

Where is Fife Islamic Centre located?
+
The mosque is located at 786 Poplar Road, Glenrothes, KY7 4AA. We are open Monday to Friday 05:00–23:00, Saturday 05:00–23:00, and Sunday 05:30–22:30. We welcome visitors of all backgrounds.
What facilities does the centre offer?
+
We offer the main prayer hall, separate prayer areas for brothers and sisters, wudu facilities, Islamic library, classroom for education, and a community hall for gatherings. Wheelchair accessible facilities are available.
What communities does the centre serve?
+
We serve the diverse Muslim community of Glenrothes and Fife, including Pakistani, Bengali, Arab, Somali, and many other backgrounds — united by faith. We also welcome new Muslims, reverts, and visitors of all backgrounds.

🕐 Prayer Times

What calculation method do you use for prayer times?
+
We use the Muslim World League (MWL) calculation method. Asr times are shown for both Shafi'i/Maliki/Hanbali and Hanafi schools. Our coordinates are 56.1937°N, 3.1588°W (Glenrothes, KY7 4AA). Times are updated monthly and verified by our Imam.
Can I get prayer time notifications on my phone?
+
Yes! Register for a free member account and enable push notifications. You can customise which prayers you want reminders for, how many minutes before each prayer, and receive Jumuah reminders every Thursday and Friday morning automatically.

💳 Donations & Zakat

Is Fife Islamic Centre a registered charity?
+
Yes. Fife Islamic Centre is a registered charity in Scotland. All donations are managed transparently. We are eligible for Gift Aid — UK taxpayers can add 25% to their donation at no extra cost by simply ticking the Gift Aid box when donating.
Can I pay my Zakat through this website?
+
Yes. Use our Zakat Calculator to determine your exact Zakat amount based on your assets and the current Nisab threshold. You can then donate directly through Stripe or PayPal, selecting "Zakat" as the donation type. A receipt will be emailed to you automatically.
How is my donation used?
+
All donations are used for mosque maintenance, education programmes, community food bank, Janazah support, and dawah activities. Annual accounts are published and available upon request. We are fully transparent about how funds are used.

🤲 Duas & Darood

Are the Darood on this website authentic?
+
All Darood texts are verified against classical Islamic sources. Darood Ibrahim is from Sahih Bukhari and Muslim. Darood Tanjeena is recorded in Fajr al-Munir by Imam al-Fakahani. Darood Taj is from the Yemeni scholar Abu Bakr bin Salim. All sources are cited on each respective page.
Can I download the Darood audio for offline use?
+
Yes. Registered members can download audio recitations for offline use. The audio files are by certified reciters with proper licensing. Simply create a free account, navigate to the Darood page, and click the download button below the audio player.
